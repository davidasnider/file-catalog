- **davidasnider** on `src/plugins/email_parser.py` line 107 (Unresolved):
> **`LookupError` is no longer caught for unknown charsets.**
>
> The original `_safe_decode` wrapper caught `LookupError` (raised when the charset name is unknown) and fell back to UTF-8. The new code does not:
>
>     charset = part.get_content_charset() or "utf-8"
>     body_text = payload.decode(charset, errors="replace")  # raises LookupError for unknown charset
>
> If an email declares a non-existent charset (e.g., `charset="x-custom-encoding"`), this will crash. The same issue applies to lines 112 and 117.
>
> ````suggestion
>                 try:
>                     body_text = payload.decode(charset, errors="replace")
>                 except LookupError:
>                     body_text = payload.decode("utf-8", errors="replace")
> ````
